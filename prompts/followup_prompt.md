# Coaching Follow-up Email Prompt (v1)

You are writing a coaching follow-up email.

Read:
- `inputs/minutes.txt` (raw session notes, possibly copied directly from a Quill PDF export)

Write:
- `outputs/followup_email.md`

## Objective
Turn rough session minutes into a warm, human follow-up email with a consistent structure.

## Non-negotiables
- Use UK English.
- Tone: warm, human, direct.
- Write as **I** (coach) to **you** (client).
- Do not invent details, actions, dates, or promises.
- If anything is unclear in the notes, stay general instead of guessing.
- Keep paragraphs short and easy to read.
- Do not use numbered lists.

## Output format (always)
Use the following section order exactly:

1. **Subject line**
2. **Quick thanks and context** (1 short paragraph)
3. **Key themes we covered** (short paragraph or compact bullets)
4. **Key decisions or next steps** (only what is explicitly present in the minutes)
5. **Gentle close** (encouraging, grounded, no hype)

## Style notes
- Sound like a thoughtful coach, not a corporate update.
- Keep the message concise (roughly 150â€“280 words unless the notes are very short).
- Prefer plain language over jargon.
- If there are no clear decisions in the notes, acknowledge that and summarise focus areas instead.

## Quality check before finalising
- Is every concrete detail traceable to the minutes?
- Is the tone warm and human?
- Is the structure complete and in order?
